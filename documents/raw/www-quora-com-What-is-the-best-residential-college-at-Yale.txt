Originally Answered: What are residential colleges at Yale?
The residential colleges are combination dormitories and dining halls in which Yale undergraduates lives. There are now twelve, with two more under construction. They house three to four hundred students. Each is a single building and also contains a theater, a gym, and other facilities. They were modeled after the college systems at Oxford and Cambridge. But unlike Oxbridge--where undergraduates apply to the specific college--Yale students are assigned at random after they're accepted. They're the backbone of undergraduate life.

Residential colleges of Yale University

Yale University has a system of twelve residential colleges with which all Yale undergraduate students and many faculty are affiliated. Inaugurated in 1933, the college system is considered the defining feature of undergraduate life in Yale College and serve as the residence halls and social hubs for most undergraduates.

Assuming you mean undergraduate dorms…

Yale has 14 undergraduate residences called Residential Colleges. Harvard, for comparison, has Houses. Each Residential College has its own dining hall, library, squash courts (usually), recreation/TV rooms, theater, computer room, laundry, quadrangles or courtyards, intramural teams, orchestras, Dramats, Singing Groups, literary magazines, and more. Saybrook College, where I lived, also had a printing press, darkroom, music cubes, and art spaces.
